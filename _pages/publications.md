I'm currently working on applying a cutting edge statistical method called the "Leave One Out-Probability Integral Transform," or LOO-PIT, in a cosmological context. It is a kind of posterior predictive check. This method has a wide variety of potential applications in cosmology. In particular, I am working on determining its sensitivity to the presence of interlopers in galaxy catalogues.

My most recent paper on correcting for interlopers in galaxy catalogues for BAO analyses is now on [arxiv!](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.14210) We introduced a novel "near/far" shift into a galaxy catalogue to self-calibrate the data for the presence of small displacement (line confusion) interlopers.
